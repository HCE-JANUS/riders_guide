How Does the Program Work?

  1. Rider calls CIRTA to request a ride from Hamilton to Hancock County, or Hancock County to Hamilton County.
  2. CIRTA will make sure we have the information needed to put the rider in both systems and will book the portions of the trip with Hamilton County Express in SPARE.
  3. CIRTA will submit a trip request form and (if needed) an intake form via email to RIDE Hancock.
  4. CIRTA will call RIDE Hancock to ensure the trip request was received.
  5. Once the RIDE Hancock dispatcher confirms or denies the trip, the RIDE Hancock dispatcher will respond to the request email with the final trip details (or denial) to CIRTA at info@cirta.us. This should occur as soon as possible, but no more than 48 business hours later.
  6. CIRTA will follow up on day #2 with RIDE Hancock if the trip is not confirmed or denied.
  7. Once final trip details are received, CIRTA will call the rider to inform them of the final trip details and provide necessary information.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if a rider wants to learn more about One-Call Connect?
Provide them with CIRTA's phone number to answer their questions at 317-327-7433.
Can the driver provide change?
No. The rider must have $8.00 exact change, cash only.
How far out and how soon can a trip be booked?
Up to 2 weeks out and as soon as 48 hours in advance.
What are the earliest pick-up and drop-off times?
Drivers for HCE drive from 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM. Drivers of RIDE Hancock can be in the vehicle as early as 7:00 AM and must return to RIDE Hancock by 5:00 PM.
How long will the rider wait to hear if we can complete their request?
CIRTA will call the rider back within 48 business hours to confirm trip details or let them know we cannot complete it.
Do passengers pay $8 each time they board a bus?
No. Passengers pay $8 for one full trip to the initial pick-up driver. If they make a return trip, they will pay $8 again ($16 round trip total).
How will a passenger know that their trip is confirmed?
Riders will receive a call back from a CIRTA employee within 48 business hours to confirm their trip. The employee working in the office will check for pending requests each morning.
How long will the driver wait for the rider?
5 minutes before they are marked as a "no show."
What if a driver does not show up?
CIRTA will call the agency that did not arrive.
Will the passengers be left to wait alone for the next bus?
No. Drivers will stay with the passengers until the transfer is complete.
Are children allowed to ride?
Yes. Infants and children up to age 10 ride for free. A car seat is required and must be installed by the parent.
Why can't I go to other counties with the program?
The hope is to someday have a program like this across Central Indiana. In this initial stage, Hamilton and Hancock County have agreed to partner with CIRTA to see how it goes.
